World Cup 'Nations'
Technically "Nations" compete in the world cup, not countries
There are several teams who compete for a place in the World Cup (and indeed compete in the World Cup) who are not recognized as official states in terms of the UN definition. The most obvious in our tournament this year is Scotland, part of the United Kingdom. You will also note that there is an "England" team, not a UK team. Wales and Northern Ireland are also competitors though they did not qualify this year. However Curaçao, a territory of the Netherlands, did qualify! This is why we call the teams "Nations" instead of "countries."
